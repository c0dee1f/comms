POST_001: VERNAM FILE ENCRYPTION

I am always on the lookout for simplified means of data encryption that can aid adoption for new users. So I was delighted to discover that PrivacyTools.io recently released a tool named VERNAM on GitHub, which can encrypt any file on the fly. You can test it out at VERNAM.app or visit the repo to examine the code.

One aspect I admire is that PrivacyTools.io encourages everyone to host copies of the tool. The process is quick and easy using GitHub and a web browser. Once you have logged in and forked a copy of the VERNAM repo to your account, go to your repo's Settings and click "Pages" from the "Code and automation" section in the left sidebar. Under "Build and deployment", select "Deploy from a branch" from the "Source" dropdown menu, then select "main" and "/(root)" from the "Branch" dropdown menus. Click the "Save" button and, after a minute or two, refresh the "Pages" settings screen.

If all went well, at the top of the screen you should see a live URL leading to your very own hosted copy that looks like this. Congratulations!
